Media Objects

Basic Media Object

This example shows media objects aligned to the left of the content and aligned to the right of the content.

The <div> element uses the .media class to create a container for the media object.

The .media-heading class is used for the headings.

The text is placed inside the container using the media-body class.

.media-left aligns images to the left, while .media-right aligns images to the right.

Example:

El Greco

El Greco was born in the Kingdom of Candia, which was at that time part of the Republic of Venice, and the center of Post-Byzantine art. He trained and became a master within that tradition before traveling at age 26 to Venice, as other Greek artists had done.[2] In 1570 he moved to Rome, where he opened a workshop and executed a series of works. During his stay in Italy, El Greco enriched his style with elements of Mannerism and of the Venetian Renaissance taken from a number of great artists of the time, notably Tintoretto. In 1577, he moved to Toledo, Spain, where he lived and worked until his death. In Toledo, El Greco received several major commissions and produced his best-known paintings.

Lucien Freud

He was born in Berlin, the son of Jewish architect Ernst L. Freud and the grandson of Sigmund Freud. His family moved to Britain in 1933 to escape the rise of Nazism. From 1942-43 he attended Goldsmiths College, London. He enlisted in the Merchant Navy during the Second World War.

Top, Middle, and Bottom Alignment

Media objects can be aligned at the top, middle, or bottom using the media-top, media-middle, or media-bottom classes.
